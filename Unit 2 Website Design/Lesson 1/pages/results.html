Results

Since concentrations of less than 10% permachrome ink significantly affected C. elegans populations, with noticeably smaller populations, these results indicate that permachrome ink may indeed be injurious, and warrant further analysis. This is also consistent with pre-existing research into ink ingredients like xylene, toluene, and cresol being potent toxins (“Permanent Marker Ink”). Accordingly, the insight these results offer is valuable to better understanding the toxicity of permachrome ink and its potential effects and dangers. Further research can be done to explore the effects of permachrome ink on specific tissues and organs to derive a more comprehensive insight into the ink and its safety.
