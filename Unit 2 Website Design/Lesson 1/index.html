The Effect of Permachrome Ink on C. Elegans Lifespan

2021-2022

Background

Permanent markers are one of the most popular stationery products in the United States. As such, safety certifications like the AP designation indicate the safety of the products’ ink, but this designation encourages a false sense of security that may not genuinely reflect the potential dangers of the product. Most markers use permachrome ink, which contains dangerous chemicals like xylene, toluene, and cresol. This ink may not have been tested directly on the human body, so it warrants further insight to guarantee its safety.
