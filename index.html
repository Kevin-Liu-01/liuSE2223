The Effect of Permachrome Ink on C. Elegans Lifespan

2021-2022

Background

Permanent markers represent more than $317 million worth of products. The vast majority of permanent markers receive Approved Product (AP) Seals to designate their safety, indicating that the marker ink has been tested and that toxins were not found in sufficient quantities to be injurious to people. However, this certification only applies when the product is being used for the intent it was created. This does not necessarily guarantee that markers were specifically tested for direct exposure to the human body.

Rationale

Permanent markers are one of the most popular stationery products in the United States. As such, safety certifications like the AP designation indicate the safety of the productsâ€™ ink, but this designation encourages a false sense of security that may not genuinely reflect the potential dangers of the product. Most markers use permachrome ink, which contains dangerous chemicals like xylene, toluene, and cresol. This ink may not have been tested directly on the human body, so it warrants further insight to guarantee its safety.

Research Practicum

The results from this study are not a final conclusion regarding the safety of permachrome ink, but do indicate that further research should be done testing the toxicity of AP-certified permachrome ink to ensure its safety. Since concentrations of less than 10% permachrome ink significantly affected C. elegans populations, with noticeably smaller populations, these results indicate that permachrome ink may indeed be injurious, and warrant further analysis.
